Churro Recipe

Churros are a delicious fried-dough pastry that originated in Spain and Portugal. They are typically enjoyed as a sweet treat, often dusted with cinnamon sugar and served with a dipping sauce like chocolate or caramel. Here's a simple recipe to make churros at home:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • oil for frying
  • butter

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the milk, sugar, vanilla extract, and salt. Heat over medium heat until the mixture begins to simmer.
  2. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the flour until a dough forms.
  3. Allow the dough to cool slightly before transferring it to a piping bag fitted with a star tip.
  4. Heat oil in a deep fryer or large pot to 375°F (190°C).
  5. Pipe the dough into the hot oil, cutting it into desired lengths with scissors. Fry until golden brown, about 2-3 minutes per side.
  6. Remove the churros from the oil and drain on paper towels. While still warm, toss them in cinnamon sugar or serve with melted butter for dipping.
